MINDFULNESS MEDITATION

With Jean Vitrano

In this 30 Minute class, you will be guided in Mindfulness Meditation. With a focus on the breath, the practice will bring us into the present moment, strengthen our concentration, and allow our body and mind to rest right here, right now. By stopping, focusing on our breathing and observing our thoughts, feelings, and sensations, we gain clarity, spaciousness and the ability to stay present to whatever arises without reacting habitually. By practicing with the support of community, our commitment to living mindfully is strengthened.

Mindfulness In Daily Life

With Jessica O'Niell

The practice of mindfulness leads to the development of a clear, contented mind; one that comes to understand that happiness and peace aren't found in outside circumstances, but lie within us, just waiting to be awakened. In this class, we will explore a number of different formal mindfulness meditation practices, then follow with a discussion of how we might take the practice off the cushion and into everyday life.

Tai Chi

Tai Chi has been called "Meditation in Motion" and is beneficial for health (especially stress reduction), meditation and self defense. Tai Chi is an ancient Chinese art involving a series of movements performed in a slow, focused manner accompanied by deep breathing. In Tai Chi we use the philosophy of the Taoist Yin Yang to balance ourselves and use "pushing hands" with a partner (Tui Shou) to understand our relationship in the universe. The class commences with meditation to ready the body for practice. Then a series of Qigong exercises are done to balance our energies. The main focus of the class is then practicing the Tai Chi form (Yang form), continually learning more movements. followed by Tui Shou (pushing hands) and finishing with a meditation. (wear loose, comfortable clothing)

Meets Tuesdays 6:15pm to 6:45pm

Qi Gong

Qi Gong is an ancient Chinese Art using movement, visualization and Healing sounds to cultivate and move life force energy known as “Qi”. Loosely translated “Qi Gong” means “Energy Cultivation”. Mike has found Qi Gong to be one of the most effective and efficient ways to put the body mind and spirit into alignment to promote Health and Healing.

Meets Tuesdays 6:15pm to 6:45pm
